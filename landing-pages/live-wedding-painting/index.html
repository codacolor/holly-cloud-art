Nice to meet you // I'm Holly

An artist, quietly at the edge of your day.

I set up somewhere unobtrusive at your ceremony or reception and paint the moment as it unfolds. Your guests watch it come to life over the evening, and you leave with an original painting of your wedding. Not a print. Not a photo. A one-of-one piece you will hang for the rest of your life.

I paint clouds, light, and atmosphere for a living. A wedding is the most human version of that, and it is my favorite thing to paint.

A scene, not a portrait

The most beautiful weddings aren't remembered as a pose.

Most live wedding painters paint your faces mid-ceremony. It is a kind idea, but portraits are hard to get right, and they often end up closer to caricature than art. Lovely for a season, then quietly moved to a spare room.

A whole house of your own wedding photos runs into the same trouble. Beautiful to you, a little much for everyone else.

So I paint the scene instead. Your venue in the golden light. The sky at the moment you said yes. The atmosphere of the entire day, held in a single frame.

Step back and it reads as fine art first, and your wedding second. It becomes the painting guests ask about, the one with a story only you can tell. And it is the rare wedding keepsake your children and grandchildren will actually want on their own walls, long after the photos are boxed away. One of one. Made to be handed down.

Your day deserves to be remembered

as a true work of art.

But it is about more than the beauty of the finished piece.

The entertainment your guests didn't expect.

Throughout the evening, people drift over to watch the canvas change. It becomes a gathering point, a conversation, a small piece of wonder happening in real time. Nobody has seen this at a wedding before.

A keepsake, not just coverage.

Photographers capture what happened. A live painting captures how it felt. You go home the same night with a finished original: the light, the color, the feeling of your day, held in paint.

An heirloom that outlives the trends.

Albums get put away. This hangs on your wall. Years from now it is still the first thing people ask about, and still the truest reminder of the day you started your life together.
